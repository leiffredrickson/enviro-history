The Mysis Crisis: "Invasive" Shrimp Introductions in North America

Leif Fredrickson

From 1949 until the 1980s, fisheries managers introduced mysis shrimp into hundreds of freshwater lakes in North America. The hoped that this shrimp would provide a self-sustaining food source for game fish in low-nutrient ("oligotrophic") lakes. In many cases, however, these introductions resulted in the destruction of the fisheries the shrimp were intended to bolster.
