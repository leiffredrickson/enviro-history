  • The Mysis Crisis: "Invasive" Shrimp Introductions in North America

    From 1949 to the 1980s, fisheries managers introduced mysis shrimp into hundreds of freshwater lakes in North America. The hoped that this shrimp would provide a self-sustaining food source for game fish in low-nutrient ("oligotrophic") lakes. In many cases, however, these introductions resulted in the destruction of the fisheries the shrimp were intended to bolster.
